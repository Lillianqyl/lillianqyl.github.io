User Experience & Behaviour Research

Improving Negative Experience of Cyberbullying Victimisation on Social Media

PhD research at the University of Melbourne

My PhD research builds upon psychology, criminology, information systems and human-computer interaction theories to study the associations between the negative consequences of cyberbullying victimisation and downstream behavioural responses. The ultimate objective of the thesis is to attain applicable social media design recommendations for promoting effective coping and prosocial response behaviours. Social media should not merely serve as a breeding ground for cyberbullying risks; rather, social media needs be leveraged as tools in mitigating cyberbullying victimisation.

Unpacking Algorithmic Tension and Unethical Social Media Engagement

Resarcher at the University of Melbourne

With the opportunity to lead a research team, we have gathered preliminary findings on users' love-hate tension against algorithm-driven social media, in which users have been actively self-initiating collective action for higher anonymity to ease such tension. We are currently working on an extended design to unpack how the love-hate tension particularly leads to the engagement of unethical content and behaviour on social media. More importantly, we aim to identify the individual social and technical factors that mitigate unethical engagement and thus, propose design recommendations accordingly.

Jun 2023 - Current

Enhancing Human-AI Collaboration in Teams at Workplace

Resarcher at the University of Melbourne

Marketing campaigns and social media influences have resulted in an overhype for AI technologies, leading to a disconfirmation of expectations while interacting with AI agents. We are currently exploring the factors that hammer professionals' willingness to collaborate with AI agents in the workplace. Notably, we are pioneering in exploring the difficult dynamics at a team level using behavioural experiments. The objective of this research is to contribute design recommendations for AI at work as well as practical insights for AI marketing and education.

Jan 2024 - Current

Tackling Gender Effects in Human-AI Collaboration in Software Development

Researcher at Deakin University

Gender inequality, along with explicit and implicit gender bias has posed challenges for women and minority genders in the software development workplace. While there has been a growing popularity of integrating AI-powered code generation tools into daily workflows of software engineering, the adoption rate of code recommendations generated by AI remains low. We are exploring gender effects and gendered designs embedded in the various components of a social-technical system that influence the adoption rate of code advice and software engineers' decisions to collaborate with AI agents.

UX Design and Development Output

Experiment Platform to Test Cyberbullying Victimis Behaviour

PhD research at the University of Melbourne

I designed and developed an experiment platform using Python Django, HTML and CSS, to support my PhD research. The design and development processes require careful consideration of the sensitive, ethical and risky characteristics of targeted users - cyberbullying victims, while still ensuring effectiveness in simulating the social media interaction as in real life. This platform is the first of its kind to receive ethics approval from the university and has been used in experiments to explore genuine behaviours under sensitive contexts. It has been methodologically tested to be effective in researching subsequent behavioural responses of cyberbullying victims with flexible manipulations of social media artefacts and designs.

Northern Health X Master of Information Systems (eHealth) Project at the University of Melbourne
